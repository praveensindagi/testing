    who we are

    The Centre for Learning and Evaluation of Results (CLEAR) is a global initiative coordinated by the World Bank’s Independent Evaluation Group aimed at strengthening developing countries’ capacities in monitoring & evaluation (M&E). CLEAR centres are spread across the globe with regional presences in Anglophone Africa, Francophone Africa, Brazil and Lusophone Africa, Latin America and the Caribbean, East Asia, and South Asia.

    CLEAR South Asia is hosted by the Abdul Latif Jameel Poverty Action Lab office in South Asia (J-PAL South Asia at IFMR). We began operations in 2011, and have thus gained experience in evaluation capacity development (ECD) in the region with diverse stakeholders like government, donors, NGOs, implementing organisations, and academic institutions.

    what we do

    CLEAR South Asia strengthens M&E systems by building capacity in the region for better monitoring, primary data collection, and rigorous evaluation of on-going developmental programs.

    We improve existing M&E practices, systems, and functions through capacity development training and workshops, technical advisory services, and knowledge exchange and dissemination events. We work towards institutionalising the usage of M&E for decision-making through long-term government partnerships and by strengthening regional research capacity through fellowships and courses.
