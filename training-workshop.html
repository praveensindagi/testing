    Training and
    Workshops

    We provide strategic and technical advisory services to strengthen monitoring and evaluation practices and strategies.

    Government partnerships lead to a varied and customised approach to capacity building. Our multifaceted approach includes, but is not limited to: technical advisory support, evidence-based insights, custom training and workshops, policy dialogues, and knowledge sharing events.

    Read about some of our key partnerships with Governments across Asia.

    Open Enrolment Courses

    Our Open Enrollment courses are ‘open’ to all - policymakers, practitioners, researchers, NGOs, governments, students, and foundations from South Asia who work on M&E

    Evaluating Social Programmes

    Evaluating Social Programmes explores how randomised evaluations can be used to rigorously measure impact for better development outcomes. The course covers the basics of measuring impact through randomised evaluations, discusses technical design choices, and delves into other pragmatic considerations when conducting a randomised study. It also explores tools that are critical for all programme evaluations—needs assessment, a Theory of Change, and how to measure outcomes effectively.

    ESP is conducted annually in India and is ideal for organisations interested in conducting randomised evaluations of their programmes, donors funding randomised evaluations, governments involved in evaluating state sponsored schemes/programmes, and individuals keen on understanding how to conduct randomised evaluations. For further information, please refer to the sample agenda from last year.

    Measurement and Survey Design Course

    The Measurement and Survey Design Course is designed to provide a thorough, step-by-step introduction to basic concepts in measurement theory and quantitative survey design. MSDC is conducted annually in India, and explores the process of designing and implementing effective survey instruments that yield high quality data.

    Concepts related to data collection, survey instrument design, piloting survey instruments, developing field protocols, as well as best practices related to each topic are covered in this course. The level of the course is basic to intermediate and is targeted at researchers, M&E professionals, and other individuals looking to build and/or refresh their knowledge of fundamental skills for collecting primary quantitative data. For more information, please refer to the sample agenda from this year.
